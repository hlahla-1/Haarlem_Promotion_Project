Museum Haarlem


Museum Haarlem is the city museum of Haarlem and South Kennemerland. Here you can experience the history of Haarlem and reflect it on the present.


All Haarlemmers


Experience 1000 years of the city's history and meet special Haarlemmers in a completely new permanent setting. Discover how the residents of the Spaarne cared for, fought, mourned, worked, played sports, believed, made love and celebrated. Meet Haarlemmers from the past and present, from world famous to ordinary. And see the city through their eyes. Twice a year there is a large temporary exhibition, linked to current events. There are also lectures, guided tours, city walks and children's workshops.


History Museum Haarlem


Museum Haarlem was founded on August 25, 1975 as a Historical Museum for South Kennemerland. Until 1988 there was no building to host exhibitions. The museum existed purely on paper. There were occasional exhibitions on location.
In 1988 the museum (together with the neighbors of the ABC architecture center Haarlem) was given access to a small space at the Groot Heiligland (anti-squat). Three rooms on the street side of the building were set up as a Historical Information Center. The volunteers who worked there at the time answered questions from curious visitors who wanted to know more about the history of Haarlem or Zuid-Kennemerland.
When the rest of the ground floor was added to the space in 1990, a real museum was created. The Story of Haarlem was told in the rear rooms and temporary exhibitions were placed in the rooms on the street side.
Since November 1998, the museum has the status of a recognized museum and has been designated an ANBI institution by the tax authorities. In 2005 the name changed to Historisch Museum Haarlem. And six years later, the first floor was also added to the museum. Then the transition to Museum Haarlem could be set in motion: a museum that provides visitors with broad information about the history and present of Haarlem, and stimulates and invites them to look at it in a new way.
In the meantime, the ground floor has been renovated and the Gasthuis - our entrance, shop, café and small exhibition space - has been opened and the name has changed into Museum Haarlem. After this first step, plans were developed for the renovation of the top floor where the new permanent exhibition on the history of the city and region was to be built. This was festively opened by the mayor in 2020.


History building


A major fire in 1576 destroyed parts of Haarlem's city center, including the Oude (en first) Gasthuis on the Botermarkt (certainly since 1282) and the Nieuwe of Groote, which opened in 1335 Gasthuis, whose name changed thirteen years later to St. Elisabeths Gasthuis, after the Saint Elisabeth of Thuringia. This hospital was on the current Verwulft.
The city council, which after the Reformation has the grounds of the Friars Minor Monastery expropriated, allocates this area between Kleine Houtstraat and Groot Heiligland to the regents of the St. Elisabeths Gasthuis. From 1597 the monastery was used as a hospital for the poor; wealthy citizens are still nursed at home. The Groot Heiligland was built between 1608 and 1616 twenty Gasthuis or Proveniers houses. After the renovation in 1907, twelve more remain cottages about.


Transport


From Haarlem Station


From Haarlem Station, the museum can be reached by bus with line 3 - direction Schalkwijk (get off at the Frans Halsmuseum stop).
Would you rather walk? You can reach the museum in about 20 minutes. Follow the tourist signs to the museum:
Exit the station on the center side. Walk via Kruisweg / Kruisstraat / Barteljorisstraat over the Grote Markt to the Grote Houtstraat and follow it to the crossing. There you turn left (Gedempte Oude Gracht). The Groot Heiligland is the second street on your right.


By car


The nearest parking garages are garage Houtplein, Wagenmakerslaan 1, and garage de Kamp, De Witstraat 1. Both garages are located within walking distance of the museum and are indicated on the signs of the parking reference system.


Disabled people


The museum is accessible for the disabled (no thresholds, elevator presence, disabled toilet, wheelchair). There is a disabled parking space in front of the door. With a disabled parking card, street parking is free in Haarlem.
